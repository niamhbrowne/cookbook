Bacon Cheeseburger, Garlic Butter Fries & Truffle Mayo

Ingredients Needed for this recipe...

  • 5g parsley
  • 35g Italian hard cheese
  • 90g British dry cured smoked streaky bacon
  • 30g panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 mayonnaise sachet (50ml)
  • 1 balsamic vinegar sachet (15ml)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 250g British beef mince
  • 1 pot of onion marmalade (40g)
  • 2 butter brioche buns
  • 3 white potatoes
  • 2 truffle-flavoured extra virgin olive oil sachets (16ml)
  • 50g rocket
  • 1 tomato

How to cook this dish...

  1. Preheat the oven to 220°C/ 200°C (fan)/ 425°F/ Gas 7

    Cut the potatoes (skins on) into thin chips

    Add the chips to a baking tray with a generous drizzle of vegetable oil and a pinch of salt and pepper

    Give everything a good mix up and put the tray in the oven for an initial 25-30 min or until golden and crisp
  2. While the chips are cooking, add the beef mince to a bowl with the panko breadcrumbs and a pinch of pepper

    Give everything a good mix up until fully combined (clean hands is the best way!) then shape into equal sized patties
  3. Add the burger patties to an oiled baking tray with the smoked streaky bacon

    Put the tray in the oven for 12-15 min or until the burgers are cooked through and the bacon is crispy
  4. Meanwhile, peel and finely chop (or grate) the garlic cloves

    Chop the parsley roughly (including the stems)

    Chop a large knob of butter into small cubes

    Peel the Italian hard cheese with a peeler until you end up with a pile of cheese shavings
  5. Once the chips are golden, remove them from the oven and push to one side of the tray

    Add the chopped butter, garlic and parsley to the chips and gently toss everything together – these are your garlic butter fries

    Add the brioche buns to the other side of the tray

    Return the tray to the oven for 2-3 mins or until the butter has melted and the brioche is warmed through
  6. Once the burgers are cooked through, remove the tray from the oven and top each patty with the Italian hard cheese shavings

    Return to the oven for 2-3 min until the cheese has melted

    Tip: If your bacon is already crispy at this point, add it to a plate and set aside
  7. Roughly chop the tomato[es]

    Wash the rocket, then pat it dry with kitchen paper

    Reserve a handful of rocket and dress the remaining rocket and the chopped tomato with a drizzle of olive oil and the balsamic vinegar

    Add the truffle oil to a bowl along with mayo and a generous grind of black pepper and mix it all together – this is your truffle mayo

    Spread each brioche base with a dollop of onion marmalade

    Add the cheesy beef patties followed by the crispy bacon, a spoonful of truffle mayo and top with the reserved rocket

    Serve the dressed salad and garlic butter fries to the side with any remaining truffle mayo

    Enjoy!
