Festive Pulled Chicken Loaded Fries

Ingredients Needed for this recipe...

  • 80g trimmed fine green beans
  • 125g mozzarella ball
  • 30g panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 x 125g British chicken breast fillets
  • 1 red wine vinegar sachet (30ml)
  • 1 bag of dried cranberries (30g)
  • 1 chicken stock mix sachet (11g)
  • 1 tsp dried sage
  • 3 white potatoes
  • 1 Henderson's Relish sachet (15ml)
  • 1 carrot

How to cook this dish...

  1. Preheat the oven to 220°C/ 200°C (fan)/ 425°F/ Gas 7 and boil a full kettle

    Cut the carrot[s] and potatoes (skins on) into chips

    Add them to a baking tray with a generous drizzle of vegetable oil and a pinch of salt

    Give everything a good mix up and put the tray in the oven for 25-30 min or until golden and crispy – these are your root veg fries
  2. Meanwhile, add a pinch of salt to a small bowl with 1 tbsp [2 tbsp] boiling water and stir until the salt has dissolved

    Add the red wine vinegar and the dried cranberries and set aside to pickle – these are your pickled cranberries

    Cut a piece of tin foil and add the green beans to the middle with 1 tsp [2 tsp] cold water and scrunch the edges together to form a sealed parcel
  3. Add half of the chicken stock mix to a bowl with 75ml [150ml] boiled water and a knob of butter

    Add the dried sage and panko breadcrumbs and give everything a good mix up – this is your sage stuffing mix

    Add the stuffing mix to one side of a baking paper-lined tray and push it out until approx. the thickness of a pound coin

    Add the green bean parcel to the other side of the tray
  4. Put the tray in the oven for 15-20 min or until the stuffing is golden and crisp

    Meanwhile, heat a large, wide-based pan (preferably non-stick with a matching lid) with a knob of butter over a medium-high heat

    Once melted, add the chicken breasts and cook for 3-4 min or until golden
  5. Whilst the chicken is cooking, dissolve the remaining chicken stock mix, Henderson's Relish in 150ml [250ml] boiled water

    Once the chicken is browned, add 2 tsp [4 tsp] flour and mix well until it's coated the chicken

    Reduce the heat to low and add the chicken stock

    Cook, covered, for 12-15 min or until the chicken is cooked through (no pink meat!) and the sauce has started to thicken
  6. Meanwhile, drain and pat the mozzarella dry with kitchen paper (squeeze as much liquid out as you can)

    Tear the drained mozzarella into rough, bite-sized pieces

    Once the root veg fries are golden and crispy, push them into 2 [4] piles and top with the torn mozzarella

    Return the tray to the oven for 5-6 min or until the cheese has melted – these are your festive fries
  7. Once cooked, cut the sage stuffing into small, bite-sized pieces

    Drain the pickled cranberries

    Once done, transfer the chicken to a clean board and shred it apart, using two forks – this technique is known as ‘pulling’

    Return the shredded chicken to the pan and give everything a good mix up

    Tip: If the gravy hasn't thickened, increase the heat to high and cook for a few minutes further!

    Load up the festive fries with the pulled chicken, sage stuffing pieces and pickled cranberries

    Enjoy!
