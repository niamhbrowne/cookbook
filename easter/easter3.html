Festive Brie & Cranberry Chicken Kiev With Bacon Mash

Ingredients Needed for this recipe...

  • 90g British dry cured smoked streaky bacon
  • 30g panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 x 125g British chicken breast fillets
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 bag of dried cranberries (30g)
  • 1 tsp dried sage
  • 60g brie
  • 160g Tenderstem broccoli
  • 3 white potatoes
  • 1 British free-range egg

How to cook this dish...

  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C/ 180°C (fan)/ 395°F/ Gas 6 and boil a full kettle

    Cut the potatoes (skins on) into large, bite-sized pieces

    Add the chopped potatoes to a pot with plenty of boiled water and a generous pinch of salt and bring to the boil over a high heat

    Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium and cook for 12-15 min or until fork-tender

    Once done, drain and return to the pot to steam dry
  2. Reboil half a kettle

    Add the dried cranberries to a bowl and cover them in boiled water

    Add the chicken breasts to a chopping board and cover with a sheet of cling film

    Bash the chicken breasts with a rolling pin until evenly flattened – these are your chicken escalopes
  3. Add a handful of flour to a plate with a generous pinch of salt

    Crack the egg[s] into a shallow bowl and beat with a fork

    Add the panko breadcrumbs and dried sage to another plate

    Coat the chicken escalopes in the flour, tap off the excess, then add them into the beaten egg and finally press them into the breadcrumbs firmly to evenly coat all over
  4. Once the chicken is coated, add the bacon and Tenderstem broccoli to a baking tray (use tin foil to avoid mess!)

    Drizzle the broccoli with a little vegetable oil and a pinch of salt and put the tray in the oven for 12-15 min or until the bacon is crispy and the broccoli is slightly charred
  5. Whilst the bacon is cooking, heat a large, wide-based pan (preferably non-stick) with a very generous drizzle of vegetable oil (enough to cover the base of the pan) over a medium heat

    Once hot, add the breaded chicken and cook for 4-5 min on each side or until golden and cooked through (no pink meat!) – these are your crispy chicken escalopes

    Tip: Add a drizzle more oil if your pan is looking a little dry!
  6. Whilst the chicken is cooking, peel and finely chop the garlic

    Cut the rind from the brie and then cut into small cubes

    Return the drained potatoes to the pot over a low heat

    Add a knob of butter, a splash of milk and a pinch of salt and mash until smooth

    Once crispy, chop the bacon roughly and stir through the mash – this is your bacon mash
  7. Once done, season the chicken with a pinch of salt and transfer to a plate until serving

    Wipe the pan clean with kitchen paper and return it to a medium heat with a knob of butter

    Once melted, add the chopped garlic and cook for 1 min or until fragrant

    Drain the soaked cranberries

    Once fragrant, remove the pan from the heat and add the brie and cranberries and set aside to melt

    Serve the crispy chicken escalopes with the bacon mash and roasted Tenderstem to the side

    Spoon the melted brie & cranberry sauce over the chicken

    Enjoy!
